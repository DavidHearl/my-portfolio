Milestone Project 1

Website Link

This is the first project I had completed as a part of my code institute diploma. It was the final part of the HTML/CSS Essentials module

After some frustration using a friend's website (Link), I decided it would be a good idea to re-create it for my first project.

I tried to stick to the basic design and strucutre used in the original creation and improve on it by incorporating a responsive design. The previous design also included a form which needed to be downloaded and printed out, then filled in. This was a good opportunity to embed a form directly into the website.

Although this project was completed on 27/09/2021 it is my intension to revisit it. As I have progressed through the code institute course I have gained more knowledge and skills that can be used to improve certain aspects that I struggled with when I first started programming. My main focus will be in converting the existing layout into Bootstrap so It is easier to style and is more responsive.

It is worth mentioning that it cannot be modifed until 27/09/2022 as to not interfere with the marking system

Client Feedback

  • I think the box that starts with 23/06/2020 should simply say "Covid-19 Procedures".
  • As I explained, take out the Make An Appointment button.
  • I like the New Patient button. I think it would make more sense to move that into the New Patient section on the drop down menu, after the three bullet points.
  • New Patient form - I can see that allowing people to fill out their forms on the computer makes sense but I'd prefer that the form still looked like a form rather than like a list of tick box questions. The information is probably the same, but the appearance is quite different. Rather than having to reorder the form to fit onto a phone, could the PDF form remain the same, but typing / ticking of the boxes be somehow enabled? The picture of the person could be left as it is, but patients could be instructed to complete that part of the form once they attend the clinic and have the printed out version in front of them.
  • New Patient section - I think I'd prefer the photo to move to the top of the page, before the text rather than after it.
  • About section - again, I'd prefer the photo was at the top of the page, before the text.
  • Contact section - I think I'd rearrange the order so that the map doesn't dominate as much. From the top: For Appointments / Address / Clinic Times / Map
  • I'd prefer the font to remain the same (as whatever the one Amy used on my original website) - that way there's a consistency throughout, from the logo onwards. But maybe that's a stipulation of your course - like you were saying about visually impaired people etc? - so maybe you use your font on your portfolio version, but we revert back for the actual website?
  • There's a couple of spelling / punctuation corrections, but they can be sorted out later. Maybe they're my errors anyway!
  • The footer looks good with the GCC and BCA logos in it. It should read "BCA member... " (no full stop, small M). My registration number would make more sense if it said "GCC Registration number".
  • I think the last bit at the bottom of the Web page should be the footer, so the "To make am appointment" bit would be better above the footer rather than below it.
